Why Secure Messaging Needs More Than End-to-End Encryption

End-to-end encryption is the minimum admission price for a serious messaging system, not the end of the design problem. If a service only answers the question “can the server read the ciphertext?” it has not yet answered several questions that matter just as much in practice: who can see the communication graph, what survives on the device, what happens when a relay is compromised, how downgrade or replay boundaries fail, and whether tampering leaves any durable evidence.
